========================================
LUBRICATION SYSTEM OVERVIEW
========================================

The X15 lubrication system delivers pressurized oil to all critical engine
components. The gear-driven oil pump is mounted at the front of the engine
and draws oil from the sump through a pickup screen.

Key components:
- Oil pump (gear-driven, high volume)
- Oil pressure relief valve (limits max pressure)
- Oil filter (full-flow, spin-on type)
- Oil filter bypass valve (opens if filter is clogged)
- Oil cooler (plate-and-frame, cooled by engine coolant)
- Oil pressure sensor (on main oil gallery)
- Piston cooling nozzles (spray oil on piston underside for cooling)

Normal oil pressure specifications:
  Hot idle (90 deg C oil temp):    138-172 kPa (20-25 psi) MINIMUM
  Normal operating (1200 RPM):    276-414 kPa (40-60 psi)
  High RPM (1800+ RPM):           345-483 kPa (50-70 psi)

  Warning threshold:  <138 kPa (20 psi) at idle — yellow lamp
  Critical threshold: <103 kPa (15 psi) at idle — red lamp + derate
  Shutdown threshold: <69 kPa (10 psi) — engine protection shutdown

========================================
FAULT CODE 100 - ENGINE OIL PRESSURE LOW
========================================

SPN: 100 | FMI: 1
Lamp: Red | Triggers derate: Yes | Triggers shutdown: Yes

CRITICAL: Do NOT restart engine until root cause is identified.
Running with low oil pressure causes catastrophic bearing failure.
This is a genuine safety fault — never dismiss or clear without diagnosis.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS WHEN CODE 100 OCCURS:
1. Shut down engine immediately if not already shut down
2. Do NOT attempt restart to see if code clears on its own
3. Check oil level before any further diagnostics

DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURE - CODE 100:

Step 1: Check oil level — always do this first
  - Locate dipstick (right side of engine, yellow handle)
  - Wipe clean, reinsert fully, remove and read
  - Normal range: between MIN and MAX marks on dipstick
  - Low oil is the most common cause of code 100
  - X15 oil capacity: approximately 38 liters (10 gallons) with filter
  - If low: inspect for leaks before adding oil
  - Do NOT overfill — overfill causes oil aeration and foam, dropping pressure
  - If oil level is correct: proceed to Step 2

Step 2: Verify with INSITE before condemning components
  - Connect INSITE diagnostic software
  - Navigate to: Engine > Lube System > Oil Pressure
  - Compare INSITE reading to the specifications above
  - If INSITE shows normal pressure but code is active: sensor fault (Step 3)
  - If INSITE confirms low pressure with correct oil level: mechanical (Step 4)

Step 3: Oil pressure sensor test and replacement
  - Part: CUM-4089916 ($48, in stock)
  - Location: main oil gallery, passenger side of block near front
  - Tools: 27mm deep socket, thread sealant
  - Remove sensor and install manual test gauge in sensor port
  - Start engine briefly — 10 seconds maximum
  - Compare manual gauge reading to INSITE
  - If manual gauge reads normal pressure: replace sensor
  - If manual gauge confirms low pressure: sensor is accurate, go to Step 4
  - Install new sensor with thread sealant — torque 40 N·m (30 lb-ft)

Step 4: Mechanical low oil pressure diagnosis

  Oil filter bypass:
  - Replace oil filter first — it is cheap and fast
  - Part: CUM-3102765 ($28, in stock)
  - A stuck-open bypass valve allows unfiltered oil to bypass the filter
  - It also reduces system pressure — replacing filter resolves this
  - Recheck pressure after filter replacement before further diagnosis

  Oil condition check:
  - Drain small sample of oil into clear container
  - Milky or frothy oil: coolant contamination — internal leak, stop diagnosis here
  - Very thin, watery oil: fuel dilution — injector leak
  - Sludgy/dark with grit: severely overdue oil change — change oil and recheck

  Oil pump inspection:
  - If pressure remains low after filter replacement:
  - Remove oil pan (2-3 hour job, requires oil drain)
  - Inspect pump gears for scoring or wear
  - Check pump intake screen for blockage
  - Measure pump clearances with feeler gauge
    Tip clearance specification: 0.025-0.076 mm (0.001-0.003 in)
    Side clearance specification: 0.025-0.102 mm (0.001-0.004 in)
  - Outside specifications: replace oil pump assembly

  Main bearing assessment:
  - Worn main bearings allow oil to escape faster than pump can supply
  - Symptom: low pressure at all RPM ranges despite correct oil level
  - This requires engine overhaul — escalate to senior tech immediately
  - Do not continue operating equipment in this condition

========================================
OIL CHANGE PROCEDURE
========================================

Service interval (off-highway severe service): Every 500 hours or 1 year
Oil specification: CES 20078 compliant
  - SAE 15W-40 for ambient temps above -18°C (0°F)
  - SAE 10W-30 for ambient temps -18°C to -29°C (-0°F to -20°F)
System capacity: 38 liters / 10 gallons with filter

Procedure:
1. Warm engine to operating temperature (improves oil drainage)
2. Shut down — allow 5 minutes for oil to drain back to sump
3. Position drain pan under drain plug (drain plug: 40mm socket)
4. Remove drain plug — allow full drainage, 10-15 minutes minimum
5. Remove old oil filter (use filter wrench if needed)
6. Pre-fill new filter with clean oil before installation
7. Apply thin film of clean oil to new filter gasket
8. Install filter hand-tight plus 3/4 turn — do not use wrench to install
9. Install drain plug with new sealing washer
   Torque: 100 N·m (74 lb-ft)
10. Add correct quantity of fresh oil via fill tube (top of valve cover)
11. Start engine — check for leaks at drain plug and filter
12. Verify oil pressure in INSITE (should reach normal range within 10 seconds)
13. Shut down — wait 5 minutes — recheck dipstick, top up if needed

OIL ANALYSIS PROGRAM:
For equipment over 15,000 hours, send an oil sample to a certified lab
every 250 hours. Early warning of bearing wear (copper, lead particles)
or coolant contamination allows intervention before catastrophic failure.

========================================
PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE — LUBRICATION SYSTEM
========================================

Every 500 hours:
  - Oil and filter change (as above)
  - Inspect for external oil leaks (valve cover, rear seal, front seal)

Every 2,000 hours:
  - Oil cooler inspection — check for restricted coolant flow
  - Crankcase breather inspection and cleaning

Every 6,000 hours:
  - Oil cooler pressure test
  - Review oil analysis history for bearing wear trends

Common oil leak locations on X15:
  - Valve cover gasket (top of engine — relatively easy repair)
  - Front crankshaft seal (timing cover area — moderate repair)
  - Rear crankshaft seal (flywheel area — requires transmission separation)
  - Oil cooler housing O-rings (passenger side of block)
  - Turbocharger oil supply and drain lines

TOOLS REQUIRED FOR LUBE SYSTEM SERVICE:
  - INSITE diagnostic software
  - 27mm deep socket (oil pressure sensor)
  - 40mm socket (drain plug)
  - Oil filter wrench
  - Torque wrench (0-150 N·m range)
  - Drain pan minimum 50 liter capacity
  - Oil sample extraction kit (for oil analysis)
  - Feeler gauge set (pump clearance measurement)
