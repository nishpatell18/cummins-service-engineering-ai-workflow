SECTION 8.1 - TURBOCHARGER AND BOOST SYSTEM

========================================
TURBOCHARGER SYSTEM OVERVIEW
========================================

The X15 uses a Variable Geometry Turbocharger (VGT) to optimize boost pressure
across the full RPM range. Unlike fixed geometry turbos, the VGT adjusts its
internal vane position to control exhaust gas flow, allowing precise boost control
at low RPM and protecting the engine at high RPM.

Key components:
- VGT turbocharger assembly
- VGT actuator (electronic — controls vane position)
- Charge Air Cooler (CAC) — cools compressed intake air
- CAC inlet and outlet hoses and boots
- Boost pressure sensor (intake manifold)
- Compressor outlet temperature sensor
- Turbo oil supply line (pressurized engine oil lubricates turbo bearings)
- Turbo oil drain line (returns oil to sump by gravity)

Normal boost pressure specifications (X15 at rated power):
  Idle:            Near atmospheric (0-5 kPa above ambient)
  Light load:      50-100 kPa (7-15 psi) above atmospheric
  Medium load:     100-150 kPa (15-22 psi) above atmospheric
  Full load:       150-200 kPa (22-29 psi) above atmospheric

Normal compressor outlet temperature:
  Light load:      Below 150 deg C (300 deg F)
  Heavy load:      150-180 deg C (300-356 deg F)
  Warning:         Above 205 deg C (400 deg F) — Code 1127 activates

========================================
FAULT CODE 102 - INTAKE MANIFOLD PRESSURE LOW (LOW BOOST)
========================================

SPN: 102 | FMI: 1
Lamp: Yellow | Triggers derate: Yes

Low boost means the turbo is not producing expected pressure. The engine
derates because without adequate air, fuel cannot be burned efficiently.

DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURE - CODE 102:

Step 1: Check for boost leaks — most common cause
  Boost leaks allow compressed air to escape before reaching the engine.
  Even a small crack causes significant power loss and low boost codes.

  Inspection points (in order, intake side to engine):
  a. Air filter and air filter housing — check for damage or loose clamps
  b. Air intake tube from filter to turbo inlet — check all clamps
  c. Turbo compressor outlet boot — most common leak point, check carefully
  d. CAC inlet hose and clamps
  e. CAC core — inspect fins for damage, check end tanks for cracks
  f. CAC outlet hose and clamps
  g. Intake manifold boots and connections

  Boost leak test procedure:
  - Seal air intake at filter housing with a rubber plug
  - Apply compressed air at 69 kPa (10 psi) to CAC outlet port
  - Listen and feel for air leaks at all connection points
  - Spray soapy water on suspect areas — bubbles indicate leak
  - Repair all leaks found before proceeding

Step 2: Check VGT actuator operation
  - Connect INSITE
  - Navigate to: Engine > Turbocharger > VGT Actuator Test
  - Command VGT to minimum position (0%) — record actual position
  - Command VGT to maximum position (100%) — record actual position
  - Actuator should move smoothly through full range in under 3 seconds
  - If actuator does not respond: electrical fault (Step 3)
  - If actuator moves but boost is still low: turbo mechanical fault (Step 4)

Step 3: VGT actuator electrical diagnosis
  - Part: CUM-4089174 ($640 — requires approval, not in stock — order required)
  - Disconnect actuator electrical connector
  - Measure actuator resistance: specification 8-16 ohms
  - Check 5V reference signal at ECM pin (refer to wiring diagram)
  - Check ground circuit continuity
  - If electrical checks out but no movement: replace actuator
  - Note: Actuator calibration required after replacement using INSITE

Step 4: Turbocharger mechanical assessment
  Turbo shaft play check:
  - Remove air intake pipe from turbo compressor inlet
  - Grip compressor wheel and check for radial play (side to side)
  - Acceptable radial play: up to 0.5mm (0.020 in)
  - Axial play (in/out): up to 0.1mm (0.004 in)
  - Excessive play indicates worn turbo bearings — replace turbo assembly

  Visual inspection:
  - Check compressor wheel for blade damage (even small nicks cause imbalance)
  - Check turbine side for oil coking on blades (indicates oil starvation)
  - Check for blade contact with housing (blades should spin freely)
  - Any blade damage: turbo must be replaced

  Oil supply check:
  - Low oil pressure causes turbo bearing failure
  - Verify code 100 (oil pressure) is not also present
  - Check turbo oil supply line for restriction or kinking

VGT ACTUATOR CALIBRATION (required after replacement):
1. Install new actuator — torque mounting bolts 10 N·m (89 lb-in)
2. Connect INSITE
3. Navigate to: Service Routines > VGT Actuator Calibration
4. Follow INSITE prompts — engine must be at operating temperature
5. Calibration takes approximately 5 minutes
6. Verify boost pressure matches specifications before returning equipment

========================================
FAULT CODE 1127 - TURBO COMPRESSOR OUTLET TEMPERATURE HIGH
========================================

SPN: 1127 | FMI: 0
Lamp: Red | Triggers derate: Yes (CM2450 only)

High compressor outlet temperature means the turbo is working excessively hard
or the charge air is not being cooled adequately by the CAC.

COMMON CAUSES:
1. Charge Air Cooler restriction or damage (most common)
2. CAC hose leak (turbo working harder to compensate)
3. High ambient temperature combined with maximum load
4. Turbo overspeeding due to VGT malfunction
5. Severe air restriction at filter

DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURE - CODE 1127:

Step 1: Check Charge Air Cooler
  - Inspect CAC fins for debris plugging (mud, insects, crop material)
  - Clean with low-pressure water or compressed air from engine side out
  - Inspect CAC end tanks for cracks (pressure test if suspected)
  - Part (if replacement needed): CUM-5268878 ($910 — not in stock, order required)

Step 2: Check for air restriction
  - Check air filter restriction indicator
  - Replace air filter if restriction indicator is red
  - Check air intake path for debris or collapsed tubing

Step 3: Check ambient and load conditions
  - High ambient temperature (>40 deg C / 104 deg F) + maximum load is a normal
    operating condition that may trigger this code temporarily
  - If code clears when load is reduced: no fault, advise operator on duty cycle
  - If code persists at normal load: proceed with CAC pressure test

CAC PRESSURE TEST PROCEDURE:
1. Disconnect CAC inlet and outlet hoses
2. Install pressure test caps on CAC connections
3. Apply 207 kPa (30 psi) compressed air to test port
4. Submerge CAC in water tank or spray with soapy water
5. Bubbles indicate internal or external leak
6. Any leak found: replace CAC (requires supervisor approval)

========================================
TURBOCHARGER OIL SYSTEM
========================================

The turbocharger bearings are lubricated by pressurized engine oil.
Oil starvation is the leading cause of premature turbo failure.

Oil supply line:
- Feeds oil from main gallery to turbo bearing housing
- Must be inspected for restriction (carbon deposits inside)
- Clean or replace if restricted — never run with restricted oil supply

Oil drain line:
- Returns oil to sump by gravity — must not be restricted
- Kinked or blocked drain line causes oil to back up into compressor housing
- Oil in intake air (blue/white smoke at startup) often indicates drain restriction

TURBO BREAK-IN PROCEDURE (after replacement or extended shutdown):
1. Disconnect fuel shutoff solenoid (prevents engine start)
2. Crank engine for 15-20 seconds — this pre-lubricates turbo bearings
3. Reconnect fuel solenoid
4. Start engine — idle for 5 minutes before applying load
5. Do NOT rev engine or apply load immediately after start
6. After service: idle engine minimum 3 minutes before shutdown
   (allows turbo to cool with oil flow before oil pump stops)

MAINTENANCE INTERVALS:
Every 500 hours:
  - Check turbo shaft play
  - Inspect oil supply and drain lines
  - Check all boost system hoses and clamps

Every 3,000 hours:
  - Turbocharger removal for inspection and cleaning
  - Replace oil supply line if carbon deposits found
  - VGT vane cleaning

TOOLS REQUIRED:
  - INSITE diagnostic software
  - Boost leak test kit: TOOL-BOOST-50
  - Compressed air source with pressure regulator
  - Dial indicator with magnetic base (shaft play measurement)
  - 10mm socket set
  - Torque wrench
  - Soapy water spray bottle
